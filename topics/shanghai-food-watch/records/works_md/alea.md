# Alea

## What this work actually does
Alea is Zee Zheng's casual downstairs sibling to Fabula, opened Nov 2024 — same chef, same Weihai Lu building, ground floor (Fabula upstairs). À-la-carte ¥168-358/dish vs Fabula's ¥888-1288 tasting menu. Western culinary technique applied to Ningbo flavors.

## Technical mechanism
- **Vertical chef-format**: two restaurants in one building, same chef, two price points, two formats. Shares infrastructure and chef-hand but targets different occasions.
- **Menu**: Sea Anemone Risotto, Hiramasa yellowtail w/ persimmon, Lumina Lamb Loin, Smoked King Salmon, Grilled Brittany Blue Lobster — Western technique foregrounded, Ningbo accents in the source materials.
- **Wine list**: French-chateau-heavy — Zheng's beverage program lead, distinct from food.
- **Room**: 6 tables, 20-seat cap, spartan but comfortable.

## Why it matters for the topic's stated goals
- Closes the same_chef + companion_pair edge between Alea ↔ Fabula that was an open gap.
- Templates the **vertical chef-format experiment**: instead of one chef opening sequential standalone concepts (e.g., Yong Fu Group's geographic expansion), Zheng stacks two formats in one building.
- Provides a casual entry-point to evaluate Zheng's cooking before committing to Fabula's ¥888+ tasting menu — useful as a "should I bother with Fabula" diligence step.

## What is reusable
- Vertical format pattern: when a chef wants to validate fine-dining ambition without losing accessible-menu revenue, the casual-downstairs / fine-dining-upstairs model is now Shanghai-templatable.
- Beverage-as-differentiator: a French-chateau wine list inside a Ningbo-flavored restaurant is a deliberate cross-category positioning move.

## What is not safely transferable
- Requires a specific real-estate setup (multi-floor building, same kitchen/back-of-house).
- One chef's bandwidth across two formats is the bottleneck — Zheng "also cooks at Fabula" is a load-bearing claim that may not survive long-term.

## Evidence quality
NOMFLUENCE review + Michelin Guide 2026 cross-reference + Fabula owners' page. Address, head chef, menu pricing, signature dishes triangulated. Editorial authority only — no 大众点评 / Michelin / Black Pearl listing for Alea specifically yet.

## Concrete next experiments or hypotheses
- Which space books out first in 2026? Alea-first signals the market prefers casual chef-driven cooking; Fabula-first signals tasting-menu prestige still dominates.
- Does Alea ever earn its own institutional recognition (Michelin Plate, Black Pearl entry-tier) independent of Fabula? If yes, vertical format is fully validated.
- Watch for copycat verticals: any 2026 opening that announces "casual downstairs + fine-dining upstairs" will be following this template.
