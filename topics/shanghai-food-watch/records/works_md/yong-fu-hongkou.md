# Yong Fu (甬府)

## What this work actually does
Shanghai's reference-standard Ningbo seafood restaurant — Michelin one-star since 2017, kept through 2026. Multiple Shanghai locations (Hongkou flagship + Huangpu sibling + Pudong "Yongfu Mini" casual).

## Technical mechanism
Private-room-dominant format, polished service, slow seafood-heavy dinners. Signature 红膏炝蟹 (raw drunken red-roe crab), 雪菜大黄鱼, 宁式鳗鲞, 苔菜小方烤.

## Why it matters for the topic's stated goals
Captures the Ningbo profile — saltier, seafood-forward, less sweet than 本帮 — which reads as sophisticated to local guests. Strong business-dinner default when Cantonese feels too obvious.

## What is reusable
Group has expanded to a casual format (Yongfu Mini 甬府小鲜 — 2026 Bib Gourmand). Same-group edge connects them.

## What is not safely transferable
Not casual. Not budget. The raw drunken crab is a specific taste many guests won't share.

## Evidence quality
High — sustained Michelin retention, broad editorial coverage, multi-year multiple-Shanghai-location stability.

## Concrete next experiments or hypotheses
Compare Yong Fu vs Xin Rong Ji 新荣记 on Ningbo/Taizhou seafood — both Michelin-one-star, similar pricing tier, different regional vocabulary. Identify the canonical Yong Fu dish a first-timer must order.
