# Taian Table (泰安门)

## What this work actually does
Shanghai's only Michelin three-star restaurant — a chef's-table tasting-menu format applying European technique to Chinese seasonal ingredients. Held three stars five consecutive years (2022-2026, including the new Shanghai-Jiangsu-Zhejiang regional edition).

## Technical mechanism
Counter + small dining room with Stefan Stiller's chef-driven format. ~15-course seasonal tasting menu. European cured-meat program + Iberico work alongside Chinese seasonal vegetable courses.

## Why it matters for the topic's stated goals
The single authority-signal apex for Shanghai fine dining. Any "best in Shanghai" framing has to grapple with Taian Table whether you recommend it or explicitly route around it.

## What is reusable
The format itself (chef counter + tasting menu + European-Chinese fusion in a small room) has been replicated across the city; Fabula (2025 one-star) is led by a former Taian Table sous chef.

## What is not safely transferable
The 1888-2588¥/person price point. Most occasions don't fit it. Not appropriate for casual, first dates, or quick visits.

## Evidence quality
High. Michelin authority signal + 5-year consecutive retention + Asia 50 Best presence + multiple long-form 公众号 coverage. Lacks fresh personal-verdict input.

## Concrete next experiments or hypotheses
Re-check 2027 Michelin retention (the streak will be tested). Cross-reference with Fabula (successor-concept candidate) — if Fabula maintains/upgrades, the chef-lineage edge will start to matter.
