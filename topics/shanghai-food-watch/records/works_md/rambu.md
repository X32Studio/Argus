# Rambu

## What this work actually does
Rambu is the November-2025 successor concept from chef Jun Nishiyama after Sage Gastro closed on Dec 31, 2025. It is a casual, à-la-carte Southeast Asian restaurant on Wuding Lu in Jing'an, drawing from Thai / Malaysian / Singaporean / Indonesian flavors. The chef-lineage chain is now complete and traceable: Ultraviolet (sous chef) → Sage Gastro (closed) → Rambu.

## Technical mechanism
- **Format pivot**: tasting-menu fine dining (Sage Gastro) → à-la-carte casual (Rambu ¥24-238/dish, ~3 dozen seats). Same chef pedigree, opposite format.
- **Team**: Nishiyama + Alice Fan (ex-Sage), Tiger (ex-Ambra), Chad (Bar Choice / Man Tang) — multi-discipline crew, not solo-chef.
- **Menu philosophy**: "reinterpret rather than recreate" SEA classics — Rendang Donut, laksa lemak soft-shell crab, sambal-butter turbot.
- **Visual identity**: baby-blue + pandan-green + yellow bar, batik & congkak decor — deliberate pivot away from any fine-dining cue.

## Why it matters for the topic's stated goals
- Closes the chef-lineage edge that was loose in iter 2's graph (sage-gastro → fabula was a placeholder; sage-gastro → rambu is the actual lineage).
- First worked example of the **closure → casual-reincarnation** pattern, contrasting with **closure → next-fine-dining-concept** (Ultraviolet brigade dispersed to Mr & Mrs Bund / Polux; Sage's Nishiyama went casual; Fabula represents a different cohort).
- Tests whether Michelin-track chef pedigree transfers to a casual format.

## What is reusable
- Chef-lineage tracking is now a structural pattern: when a Shanghai fine-dining place closes, the successor concept reveals what the chef actually wanted to cook all along. Rambu's casual SEA pivot is more authentic-to-Nishiyama than Sage's refined tasting menu was.
- Pricing structure (¥24-238/dish) is the casual-fine-dining sweet spot — accessible enough for a Wednesday, chef-driven enough to be a destination.
