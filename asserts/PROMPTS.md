## 1. Cute Mascot Logo — `asserts/logo/logo.png`

Aspect ratio: square 1:1. Attach `x32studio_top.png` as reference.

```
Create a premium cute mascot logo for "Argus", a self-iterating research intelligence engine inspired by Argus Panoptes, the hundred-eyed watcher from Greek mythology.

REFERENCE IMAGE INSTRUCTION:
The attached image is the X32 Studio mark: metallic warm gold on a deep cosmic-navy starfield. Use it ONLY as a palette and atmosphere reference. Match the deep cosmic navy background (#0a0d1c), the warm metallic gold accent (#c9a35a), and the premium mythic-tech mood. Do NOT include the letter X, the number 32, or any X32 mark.

MAIN MASCOT:
Argus is a tiny nocturnal research sprite: a cute round owlet-like creature with a soft teardrop body, small feather ears, tiny paws, and a curious face. It should feel like a clever little animal companion, not a human. The eyes are large, warm, and intelligent, with tiny metallic-gold highlights shaped like stars. The expression is alert, playful, and quietly helpful, like it is ready to stay up all night reading sources for you.

VISUAL DETAILS:
Give Argus a tiny dark-navy scholar cape that wraps around its round body like a blanket. The cape has three to five subtle peacock-eye markings along the hem, drawn as elegant metallic-gold ocelli, not creepy eyes. Add one small gold peacock-eye charm on the chest, like a button or brooch. Argus holds a miniature parchment note or glowing citation card in one paw. A few tiny gold knowledge-graph nodes orbit around its head like fireflies.

COMPOSITION:
Centered full-body mascot, floating slightly above the ground, three-quarter front view. Big readable silhouette, oversized head and small body, suitable for a logo avatar and README header. Behind Argus, create a soft circular halo made of faint gold knowledge-graph lines and star nodes, like a little research constellation. Keep the halo simple so the mascot remains the focus.

TYPOGRAPHY:
Below the portrait, centered, render only the word "ARGUS" in a classical high-contrast serif typeface, all caps, wide tracking, metallic gold. No tagline, no other text.

STYLE:
Cute premium mascot illustration, soft rounded shapes, polished digital painting, subtle paper grain, gentle glow, clean silhouette. The feeling should be "mythic research pet", "tiny night librarian", and "friendly long-term memory companion." Keep it elegant enough for a serious developer tool, but much more charming and playful than a corporate emblem.

DO NOT:
Do not make Argus human, robotic, scary, realistic, insect-like, surveillance-camera-like, or covered in literal eyeballs. Do not make it look like a generic owl logo; include the peacock-eye cape marks, citation card, and gold knowledge-graph fireflies. Do not add bright neon colors, weapons, extra characters, unreadable text, X32, or the letter X.
```

**Fallback if the mascot becomes too serious:**

```
Make Argus cuter and simpler: a tiny round owlet-sprite with feather ears, dark navy cape, gold peacock-eye brooch, a little citation card, and a few gold knowledge-graph fireflies. Bigger eyes, smaller body, softer expression, still premium and deep cosmic navy. Only the word "ARGUS" below in metallic gold serif.
```

## 2. Mascot Story Image — `asserts/comic/comic.png`

Aspect ratio: **landscape 3:2**. One single image, six panels arranged in a 3×2 grid. Attach `x32studio_top.png` as reference.

```
Generate a single six-panel editorial story image in one canvas, arranged in a rigid 3-column × 2-row grid. The story introduces Argus as a cute tiny research mascot: a nocturnal owlet-sprite that helps a human researcher build memory over weeks.

REFERENCE IMAGE INSTRUCTION:
The attached image is the X32 Studio mark: metallic warm gold on a deep cosmic-navy starfield. Use it ONLY as palette and atmosphere reference. Match the deep cosmic navy (#0a0d1c), warm metallic gold (#c9a35a), premium nocturnal mood, and subtle star-dust. Do NOT include the X32 mark, the letter X, or the number 32.

GLOBAL STYLE:
Whimsical editorial storybook meets premium tech illustration. Limited palette: deep cosmic navy, metallic gold, parchment cream, muted slate grey, with a small amount of soft dawn peach only in Panel 5. Rounded character shapes, clean linework, flat color fields, subtle paper grain, cinematic side-lighting. It should feel clever, warm, and a little magical, not childish or chaotic. No speech bubbles. No panel numbers. No brand names. No loud comic effects.

CHARACTER 1 — ARGUS THE MASCOT:
Argus appears in Panels 2, 3, 4, 5, and 6 and must look identical across panels. Argus is a palm-sized nocturnal owlet-sprite: round teardrop body, small feather ears, tiny paws, big expressive eyes with star-like gold highlights, dark navy scholar cape, metallic-gold peacock-eye brooch, and a few subtle peacock-eye markings on the cape hem. Argus is cute, alert, and competent. It moves like a small magical animal that loves research.

CHARACTER 2 — THE RESEARCHER:
A human researcher in their late 20s / early 30s, dark short tousled hair, round gold-rim glasses, slate-grey sweater over a cream undershirt. They appear in Panels 1, 2, 3, 5, and 6 and must remain visually consistent.

GRID LAYOUT:
Top row: Panel 1, Panel 2, Panel 3.
Bottom row: Panel 4, Panel 5, Panel 6.
Panels are equal size, separated by thin faded-gold dividers. Bottom margin contains only this tagline in metallic gold serif, all caps, wide tracking:
"A HUNDRED EYES. NEVER ASLEEP."

PANEL 1 — The drowning:
The researcher sits alone at a desk at night, overwhelmed by floating tabs, PDFs, notes, clocks, and fading search cards. Their face is the emotional center: tired eyes behind round glasses, one hand against the forehead. The laptop glow is cold blue; the room is deep navy. No Argus yet.

PANEL 2 — The tiny arrival:
Over the researcher's shoulder, the laptop screen shows a blank new research session. A tiny gold spark pops out from the laptop hinge, and Argus peeks over the keyboard with both little paws on the edge of the screen, as if climbing out of the research workspace. The researcher notices it with surprised curiosity. Argus looks bright-eyed and eager, holding one miniature citation card like a gift.

PANEL 3 — The pact:
Medium two-shot. The researcher types one clear line on the laptop: "I want to long-term track <topic>". Argus sits on the laptop corner, tiny paws raised like it is conducting an orchestra. From Argus's peacock-eye brooch, fine gold threads flow into the screen and become small connected nodes. The researcher's face shifts from exhaustion to hope. Argus looks delighted but focused.

PANEL 4 — The night watch:
The researcher sleeps at the desk in the lower foreground. Argus is awake on the desk, wearing tiny round reading glasses now, sorting floating papers, repos, notes, and citation cards into tidy piles. Around Argus, many miniature peacock-eye fireflies open like stars. Gold knowledge-graph threads connect the sources into a constellation above the laptop. The mood is funny and sweet: one tiny creature doing serious overnight research while the human sleeps.

PANEL 5 — The morning brief:
Soft dawn peach light enters from the left. The researcher wakes with a coffee mug and looks at a filled dashboard on the laptop: knowledge graph, markdown brief, route cards, citation records. Argus sits proudly beside the trackpad, slightly sleepy but pleased, wrapped in its tiny cape, offering one final citation card. The emotional note is relief and delight: the tool remembered, and the little mascot worked all night.

PANEL 6 — Compounding memory:
Three-week time-lapse composition. The researcher appears in three semi-transparent crisp poses around the desk: reading, typing, writing notes. Argus appears as the consistent anchor in the center foreground, perched on a stack of tiny notebooks like a captain on a ship, cape fluttering into a star-map of gold nodes and peacock-eye fireflies. The laptop shows a much denser knowledge graph with clusters and hubs. A small desk calendar shows 21 gold marks. The final image should communicate "one human, one tiny persistent watcher, memory getting deeper over time."

CANVAS COMPOSITION:
Keep Argus cute and readable in every panel. Argus should never be a tiny background decoration; make it large enough to see its face, cape, brooch, and playful body language. The visual rhythm should move from lonely human → tiny mascot appears → partnership → overnight research antics → morning delight → long-term compounding. Use the same Argus design as the logo prompt if possible.

DO NOT:
Do not make Argus human, scary, realistic, robotic, insect-like, covered in literal eyeballs, or like a generic corporate owl. Do not make it too serious. Do not make the image mainly about abstract eye symbols; the mascot's personality is the focus. Do not use bright neon colors. Do not include any text except the Panel 3 typed sentence and the bottom tagline. Do not include real product logos or recognizable app interfaces.
```

## Generation Tips

1. **Generate the mascot logo first.** Use the best result as the identity reference for Argus in the six-panel image.
2. **If Argus changes across panels**, regenerate the comic with the logo attached as a second reference image and add: *"Keep Argus's face, body shape, feather ears, cape, and brooch exactly consistent with the attached mascot logo."*
3. **If the comic becomes too serious**, add: *"Make Argus more playful and mascot-like: rounder body, bigger eyes, smaller paws, more expressive gestures, but keep the premium navy-and-gold palette."*
4. **If text is garbled**, fix in edit mode: *"Only correct the bottom tagline to 'A HUNDRED EYES. NEVER ASLEEP.' and the Panel 3 laptop line to 'I want to long-term track <topic>'; keep everything else unchanged."*
